Hydrocontest: an energy challenge

The Hydrocontest is an international naval design competition where the goal is simple: design a remote-controlled boat that carries a load while consuming the least amount of energy possible. The rules require the use of two supplied 12V 30Ah batteries, but the motorization and design are completely free.

Wave-piercing hull & active cancellation

To maximize efficiency, we have designed a wave-piercing hull, which significantly reduces drag by splitting the water's surface instead of lifting it. We are currently developing an active wave cancellation system, a technical innovation that will compensate for the bow waves generated by the forward motion.

An environmentally friendly construction

The boat's hull is made of flax fiber and bio-sourced resin, with a focus on durability. This choice of materials combines mechanical performance with a reduction in environmental impact. The whole is designed to be repairable, detachable, and partially recyclable.

Precise remote control

Our prototype is remotely controlled using an HF radio, an onboard GPS, and a high-definition camera that transmits in real-time. It measures 2.5 meters and can carry up to 100 kg of payload, with enhanced stability for critical phases.

Towards a sustainable maritime future

Through this project, we want to demonstrate that it is possible to combine performance, innovation, and ecology. The Hydrocontest pushes us to push the current limits of maritime propulsion by integrating sustainable materials and advanced technological solutions.
